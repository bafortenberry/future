Brand
Never heard of it...

If you approached the majority of marketing professionals and ask them to define a brand, you would get very similar responses. Most of them would say something along the lines of, "a brand is how people perceive and experience a company." They're wrong. A brand is more than public perception. A brand is a representation of self. It is a manifestation of beliefs and attitudes that a company holds true.

Why do you think brands are percieved how they are? It's because a company is an entity. Good people do good things and are seen in a positive light. If you can't uphold your company to set of strong standards, than you already lost the fight. A good brand is a veiled representation of a good company.

The power of branding


This is an opinion piece

A brand is, as discussed earlier, an outer reflection of company values and how they are experienced by the public. Based on this idea, everyone has a brand. Some brands have influence at a local level and can change a community. Other brands can influence national policies. There's a lot of power in that sentiment. Company's have the ability to change the way people view the world. When you have such a powerful platform, why not use it to help? As Uncle Ben would say, "with great power comes great responsibility".
